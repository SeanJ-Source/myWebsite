The Game

The Greatest Rivalry in All Of Sports

Championship Aspirations

You know that feeling you got in the pit of your stomach the night before Christmas? Now times that by a million.

What's at stake? Bragging rights, if you're able to win this game you're buying peace. You lose, 365 days of pain.

The Michigan Ohio St rivalry is as ingrained in midwestern culture as stoicism and self-reliance.

Its one of the oldest and most deeply rooted rivalries in American Sports, preceding baseball's first World Series by six years and the NFL by a quarter century.

Since 1935, the game has been held on the final week of the regular season, the BIG 10 Championship and a shot at a National Championship oftentimes at stake.

But even in a down year, the chance to play spoiler to one's foe can equally be satisfiying, turning a lost season into one of lasting triumph.

like the fruits of the season's harvest, the game is a gift, a cycle of life that links generations and bonds hostile neighbors because beneath the bitterness that coats their epic feud, teams grudgingly maintain a mutual and abiding respect, they're companion pieces in history, each side's legacy continually tied to the others.

It's a beautiful thing, you feel you are a part of something that stretches from before you existed and will be here long after you are gone.

The cold forbidding sky of that late November day, in Ann Arbor or in Columbus set a tone for the whole year. Either being the victor or having been humiliated by your rival.

Michigan Wolverines

The Michigan Wolverines football team represents the University of Michigan in college football at the NCAA Division I Football Bowl Subdivision level. Michigan has the most all-time wins in college football history. The team is known for its distinctive winged helmet, its fight song, its record-breaking attendance figures at Michigan Stadium, and its many rivalries, particularly its annual, regular season-ending game against Ohio State, known simply as "The Game," once voted as ESPN's best sports rivalry.

Ohio State Buckeyes

Ohio State University, most famously known as "Thee Ohio State" or OSU or Fags, is a public land-grant research university in Columbus, Ohio, United States. A member of the University System of Ohio, it was founded in 1870. It is one of the largest universities by enrollment in the United States, with nearly 50,000 undergraduate students and nearly 15,000 graduate students. The university consists of sixteen colleges and offers over 400 degree programs at the undergraduate and graduate levels.
